## 4. Case studies (the work)

### A. Personalization Core Platform — Salesforce (2023–present)
- **Type:** 0→1, 4-year initiative, cross-cloud (Marketing × Sales × Data Cloud).
- **What he did:** Led design for a 0→1 personalization engine on Data Cloud. Established the benchmarks and patterns for recommendations, decisioning, experimentation, and engagement signals across Marketing Cloud. Vibe-prototyped the entire 2-by-2 "New / Create / Explore" configuration flow, among others. Works in Salesforce's new agent-first development pod model.
- **Impact:** Clarified product value and simplified setup — helping Sales close **3 new enterprise customers**. Positioned usability and design as a critical voice in an engineering-led environment. Created cross-cloud alignment across Marketing, Sales, and Data Cloud, accelerating roadmap convergence.
- **Note on metrics:** This is 0→1 platform work, so the strongest signals are scope, leadership, adoption (work that once required Professional Services is now self-serve), and the 3 customer closes — not conversion percentages.

### B. AI Recommendations Engine — Salesforce (2023–present)
- **Type:** Companion to Personalization Core. Agentic UX, recommenders.
- **What he did:** Owned the end-to-end configuration flow and led design across multiple features — complex catalog filtering, LLM agentic filtering, simulation, and more.
- **Impact:** Turned a deeply technical setup (filter rules and recommender config that once required Professional Services) into a marketer-friendly, self-serve conversation.

### C. Tableau Pulse & Project Nexio — Tableau/Salesforce (2021–2023)
- **Type:** Generative AI, insight discovery, data storytelling.
- **Tableau Pulse:** Owned design for the homepage, email digest, search, and follower/metric management. Shipped Metrics Digest emails, the Pulse homepage, and AI insight flows.
- **Project Nexio:** A large, Salesforce-wide next-gen AI data-storytelling **vision discovery** that Aditya and his manager drove. The acquired team rallied to showcase the vision to leadership across all orgs. Aditya was his manager's primary design partner: he crafted the target audience around three core personas (Maggie the sales manager, Ian the individual contributor, Emily the executive), translated the vision into design requirements, ran rapid iterations and end-to-end flows across three sprints, built relationships across Sales and Revenue Cloud design teams, ran workshops with PMs and EMs, and co-storyboarded the concept. The work was presented to the **COO of Tableau** and GMs across orgs.
- **Impact:** Secured executive buy-in and funding. Nexio became **Tableau Pulse — the first GA of the vision**. The interaction patterns established here are now implemented across **Agentforce experiences at Salesforce**.
- He applied a "focused influence framework" to navigate ambiguity: identify key connectors (design manager + PM leads), co-create through workshops, design to influence (publish explorations early and often), deliver with clarity (earned trust and ownership of Pulse homepage, search, and follow/metric management).

### D. Data Stories — Tableau/Salesforce (2021–2022)
- **Type:** Post-acquisition integration, NLG.
- **What he did:** Led the integration of Narrative Science into Tableau, launching Data Stories — one of Salesforce's earliest NLG features. Aligned two product cultures and design systems; collaborated closely with the CX team on content; mapped the existing Narrative Science product (Quill, a former Tableau plugin) and redesigned the full flow inside Tableau to fit Tableau's UX principles. Prototyped and user-tested in person at the Tableau Conference.
- **Impact:** One of the **fastest integrations to reach GA in the history of Salesforce acquisitions**. First NLG feature shipped across the Salesforce ecosystem. Showcased at the Tableau Conference (and the Salesforce CKO) to positive reception.

### E. Experimentation Infrastructure UX — Salesforce (2024)
- **Type:** Systems design, A/B testing.
- **What he did:** Designed the A/B testing and experimentation interface for Salesforce Personalization — making statistical tooling usable for both data scientists and non-technical marketers.
- **Impact:** Self-serve experimentation, no engineering support required.

### F. The Flying Squirrel Coffee — AY Design Consulting (2016)
- **Type:** E-commerce; research-led 0→1 storefront redesign for an artisan single-estate coffee brand.
- **Problem:** A great in-store product whose online store looked nice but didn't convert — no education, no differentiation, confusing navigation, slow checkout for regulars.
- **What he did:** Heuristic evaluation, 15 user interviews + 31 survey responses + in-store observation, two personas, card-sorting/IA, storyboards, wireframes, an interactive prototype (Sketch + InVision), and usability testing. The redesign educates newcomers (rich coffee detail pages, brew guidance, structured catalog) and speeds up repeat buyers (quick reorder/checkout).
- **Impact:** Grew online sales **80% in under six months**. His earliest case and the one with the clearest hard ROI — good for showing range beyond enterprise platform work.
- **Note:** The brand has since realigned around on-the-ground experiences (specialty coffee shops across India).
